About Us

We are a collection of computer science students who met at HackRU IX. Inspired by the New York-Presbyterian/Morgan Stanley Children's Hospital challenge, our mission was to create this website to enable patients to find and interact with those who have similar backgrounds and experiences. Patients who are unable to physically interact with others due to limited mobility may find it difficult to develop sustained, meaningful relationships, especially with others like them. This website is intended to help extend the reach of these patients beyond their hospital room or building to connect with patients across the country. We hope that you find this website useful.


Patient Stories - An innovative platform!

While helping patients, we found it important to stop the stigma associated with certail mental illnesses. This led to the implementation of ARTICLES - a compilation of personal stories of patients. We provide a platform to patients for sharing their stories, attempting to increase overall health literacy and empathy for such patients.


How It Works

Sign in to our website and create a profile to let others know what your background is like. You can choose what health topics you are interested in talking about and we will connect you with other patients. For example, if you select "eating disorders" as a topic, we will connect you with other patients who have also selected "eating disorders." You can use our website to interact with other patients who similar experiences and are dealing with similar issues.


Why Use Our Website

It can be difficult staying in the hospital for extended durations, and we think that one of the best ways to make your stay more pleasant is talking to people who are going through similar experiences. We can help you instantly find others with similar experiences who also want to interact through our online platform. You can create and respond to posts on our messaging platform, and you can also continue the conversation by sending an email through our website to other users.
